    Brent Jay Spiner: A Brief Overview

    Born on February 2, 1949, in Houston, Texas, Brent Jay Spiner is a well-known actor, prominently recognized for playing Lieutenant Commander Data, an android, in the TV series “Star Trek: The Next Generation” (1987). He also gained praise for his role as Dr. Brackish Okun in the film “Independence Day” (1996). Brent has been actively contributing to the entertainment industry since the 1970s.

    Brent Spiner’s Wealth

    As of early 2016, Brent’s net worth has been estimated at $7 million, which he has amassed through his successful and enriching career as an actor over the years.

    Early Life and Career Beginnings

    Brent was raised in a Jewish family and attended Bellaire High School in Bellaire, Texas. He then pursued his higher education at the University of Houston, earning a degree in 1974. During his college years, Brent engaged in local theatre activities.

    Brent’s career took off in the 1970s as a stage actor, notably participating in various Broadway and off-Broadway productions, including “Sunday In The Park With George” and “The Three Musketeers”. Subsequently, he transitioned to California, where he began auditioning for on-screen roles. In 1984, he secured his first TV role in the series “The Paper Chase,” which was followed by appearances in other productions such as “Tales from the Darkside” and “One Life To Live” in the same year.

    Prior to the conclusion of the 1980s, he landed roles in TV series and films like “Crime of Innocence” (1985), “Mama’s Family” (1986-1987), and “Night Court” (1985-1987). In 1987, he attained the role of Lieutenant Commander Data in the TV series “Star Trek: The Next Generation” (1987-1994), which significantly contributed to his net worth.
