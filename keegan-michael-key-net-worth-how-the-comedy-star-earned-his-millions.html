He has also appeared in many movies and TV shows, such as The Lion King, Toy Story 4, Fargo, and The Prom. But how did he achieve this level of success and wealth? Let’s take a look at his career and how he built his net worth.

    Early Life and Education

    Keegan Michael Key was born on March 22, 1971, in Southfield, Michigan. He was adopted by a black father and a white mother, who were both social workers. He grew up in a multicultural and diverse environment, but also faced discrimination and bullying.

    He started his career as a theater actor in New York City. He graduated from the University of Detroit Mercy with a degree in theater in 1993, and then from Pennsylvania State University with a Master of Fine Arts in theater in 1996.

    Stand-Up and Sketch Comedy Career

    Key joined the cast of Mad TV midway into the ninth season in 2004. He and Jordan Peele were cast against each other so that FOX could pick one black cast member, but both ended up being picked after demonstrating great comedic chemistry. On Mad TV, Key became known for his impressions and characters, such as Coach Hines, Dr. Funkenstein, and President Obam.

    Key and his former Mad TV cast-mate Jordan Peele starred in their own Comedy Central sketch series Key & Peele, which began airing on January 31, 2012, and ran for five seasons until September 9, 2015. The show featured candid and hilarious sketches that tackled various topics, such as race, culture, politics, and pop culture. The show earned critical acclaim and won two Emmy Awards for Outstanding Variety Sketch Series and Outstanding Writing for a Variety Series.

    Key also appeared on other comedy shows, such as Whose Line Is It Anyway?, Reno 911!, Childrens Hospital, Playing House, and Friends from College.
