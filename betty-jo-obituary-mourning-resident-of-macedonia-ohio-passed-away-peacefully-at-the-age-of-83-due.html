    Betty Jo Obituary: Betty Jo Bauer Cook, a woman known for her sharp mind and meticulous nature, passed away peacefully at the age of 83. Born in Cedar City, Utah, she grew up surrounded by the beauty of Southwest Utah’s national parks and monuments. Throughout her life, Betty Jo excelled as an editor, a librarian, and a devoted mother to her four sons. She cherished her faith and served in various callings, including four missions with her beloved husband, Noal. As we honor her memory, let us reflect on the remarkable life of Betty Jo Bauer Cook.

    Obituary: Betty Jo Bauer Cook

    Betty Jo Bauer Cook, a beloved individual, passed away peacefully in her sleep at the age of 83 on December 20, 2023. Her departure was a result of natural causes associated with age. Betty Jo’s life was filled with love, accomplishments, and cherished memories that will forever be treasured by her family and friends.

    Early Life and Education

    Betty Jo Bauer Cook was born on March 9, 1940, in the picturesque town of Cedar City, Utah. She was the daughter of Myrtle and Joseph Waldorf Bauer. Growing up in Cedar City, Betty Jo attended Cedar High School and graduated in 1958. Her thirst for knowledge led her to pursue higher education, and she earned an associate degree from the College of Southern Utah (SUU) in 1960. She furthered her studies at Utah State University, the University of Utah, and Weber State University, focusing on Education and Library Services.

    Marriage and Family

    On December 28, 1960, Betty Jo embarked on a lifelong journey of love and companionship when she married her eternal sweetheart, Noal K. Cook, in the St. George Temple. Together, they built a beautiful life in West Point, Utah, where they raised their four sons: Christopher, Carter, Curtis, and Cyle. Betty Jo’s dedication to her family was unwavering, and her love and support were the foundation that nurtured their growth and happiness.

    Professional Life and Retirement

    Betty Jo’s professional life was marked by her meticulous nature, organizational skills, and sharp intellect. She served as the editor of her high school yearbook and even took on the role of editor for SUU’s yearbook during her time there. She dedicated herself to the field of education, working as a tutor at Doxey Elementary and later as the school librarian at West Point Elementary. After a fulfilling career, Betty Jo and Noal retired to St. George, Utah, where they enjoyed the fruits of their labor and the company of dear friends.

    Interests and Hobbies

    Betty Jo had a zest for life and a wide range of interests and hobbies. She found solace and joy in exploring the national parks and monuments of Southwest Utah, particularly during her teenage years when she worked summers in Zion and Cedar Breaks. As the years went by, Betty Jo and Noal developed a fondness for Ruby’s Inn at Bryce Canyon, a place they frequented and cherished. Betty Jo was an avid reader, with a particular fondness for church history books. She also took pleasure in the art of washing and ironing, ensuring that her clothes were always impeccably pressed. Additionally, she had a passion for old Westerns and could recite lines from them with ease. She and Noal even had the opportunity to visit some of the sets where these classic Westerns were filmed.

    Faith and Service

    Betty Jo’s faith in Jesus Christ was a cornerstone of her life. She had a firm testimony and actively served in various callings within her church community. Her dedication extended to serving as an ordinance worker in the St. George Temple. One of the highlights of her spiritual journey was embarking on four missions with Noal, including the St. George Temple Visitor Center Mission, Nauvoo, Illinois Mission, Washington, DC Temple Mission, and a return to the Nauvoo, Illinois Mission. These experiences brought immense joy and fulfillment to Betty Jo’s life.

    Survived by

    Betty Jo is preceded in death by her parents, sister Marsha, and sons Christopher and Cyle. She is survived by her loving husband, Noal, and two children: Carter (Julia) and Curtis (Erica). Her legacy lives on through her six grandchildren and five great-grandchildren. Betty Jo is also survived by her siblings: Christie Jensen, Lana (Bob) Fredrickson, Samuel (Debbie) Bauer, and Debra (Steve) Hardison. The bonds of family and the memories they shared will forever hold a special place in their hearts.

    Funeral Arrangements and Donations

    The funeral service to honor Betty Jo Bauer Cook’s life will be held at 11:00 am on Friday, December 29, 2023, at Lindquist’s Syracuse Mortuary, located at 869 South 2000 West in Syracuse, UT. Friends and loved ones are invited to visit with the family on Thursday, December 28, 2023, from 6:00 to 7:00 p.m. and on Friday from 9:30 to 10:30 a.m. at the mortuary. For those unable to attend in person, the funeral service will be streamed live on the mortuary’s website. Betty Jo will be laid to rest at the West Point City Cemetery. In lieu of flowers, the family kindly requests that donations be made in Betty Jo’s name to a local school, contributing to the education and growth of future generations.
