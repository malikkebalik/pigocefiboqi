    Victory Brinker- Birth, Age, Ethnicity, Siblings, Education

    Victory Brinker’s Professional Career

    Victory is the world’s youngest opera singer, having begun singing at the age of two and competing in singing and talent competitions at the age of six. She won her first competition in 2018 and went on to win several more in a row, including the 2018 Protege Intel Music Competition. The same year, she tried her hand at acting, landing her first appearance as an extra in the 2018 film “Boy Hero.” During the 2019 NBA season, she was invited by the Utah Jazz to perform the National Anthem twice during the playoffs, as well as the Pittsburgh Pirates.

    In addition, she landed her first major part as Izzy in the 2019 film “Helping Izzy.” She was a part of the “Lights and Legends” show in Pittsburgh in 2019 and immediately drew the attention of several people due to her talent. Victory also co-starred with Melissa McCarthy in the NBC channel’s Little Big Shots in 2020. She recently worked on crafting harmonies for the Dreamworks Trolls World Tour in German video, which was released in 2021.

    AGT

    The following year, she featured as a singer on the 16th season of America’s Got Talent, when she performed “Juliet’s Waltz” and garnered standing ovations and compliments from all of the judges. She became the only contestant in AGT history to receive a golden buzzer from all four judges. Victory wowed the globe once more in the quarterfinals with her lovely and heartfelt singing abilities. She wowed the judges with a spellbinding cover of “Casta Diva,” earning her a spot in the semifinals among six other contenders. Aside from that, she is now working on a few upcoming acting projects, the details of which she has not yet revealed.
