    Tokyo Revengers features a plethora of formidable warriors, but these are the best of the best. The bar for excellence has been met and then some by Tokyo Revengers. The series has made an indelible mark on the anime world, becoming a best-seller and collecting several awards across multiple categories.

    Tokyo Revengers is beloved for many reasons, but perhaps the most significant is its introduction to the concept of street gangs and the world of tough fighters in the anime genre.

    Because series creator Ken Wakui hung out with street gangs during his high school years, the series’ depiction of street gangs and their struggles is so realistic. The heroes of this series all possess extraordinary abilities, but these guys outclass everyone else.

    Shuji Hanma

    Shuji Hanma is most accurately described as a sadist. Every action he takes, no matter how deadly, is motivated only by his desire to end his boredom. He goes well with Tetta Kisaki, the largest manipulator in the series, as he derives satisfaction in seeing how his ideas turn the tables. Hanma has a callous disregard for his own life.

    Hakkai Shiba

    Nonetheless, Hakkai’s strength may put even the best fighters in the series to shame, and he is still one of the series’ lesser talked-about characters. He’s the second-in-command in the Tokyo Manji Gang’s Second Division. Hakkai’s outward demeanor belies his extreme loyalty to his gang and his willingness to risk anything to protect the people he cares about.

    Throughout the series, he fights alongside Toman and defeats multiple foes at once. A single punch from Hakkai would likely end the battle for most of his opponents. In addition, he has a more powerful older brother who is the polar opposite of himself.

    Taiju Shiba

    Taiju Shiba, Hakkai Shiba’s older brother, comes up next; talks about dealing with the devil. Taiju is heartless; he doesn’t even seem to care about his siblings or his opponents. Such a paradox, given that he is devout and a regular churchgoer. Because he possesses all the qualities of a leader, his followers put their faith in him.

    The fact that Taiju trained the 10th Generation of the Black Dragon gang attests to his extraordinary strength. Taiju is one of the select few who have managed to avoid being knocked out by Mikey’s trademark kick. Mikey took the punch on purpose, yet he’s the only person to ever knock him out with a single blow.

    Keisuke Baji

    Baji, a founding member of Toman, was first presented as a villain but eventually revealed to have a good heart. This man was so dedicated to Toman and his companions that he posed as an enemy to protect them. Baji’s keen intelligence is on full display because he is the only person except for Takemichi who can see Kisaki for who he is.

    Do you know how powerful Keisuke Baji is? To be fair, he did manage to knock out fifty Valhalla warriors despite taking a knife to the back. While he had already proven his worth by enduring for so long, he passed out from the blood loss shortly before it was over. He had proven his toughness even at a young age when he fought off the assailants and rescued Chifuyu all by himself.

    Takashi Mitsuya

    While the characters in Tokyo Revengers tend to be rude, spiteful, and gloomy, Takashi Mitsuya is as tranquil as a lake in a storm. His presence alone has the potential to create a peaceful atmosphere. Mitsuya would try to avoid fights wherever possible, but when necessary, he would not hesitate to annihilate his opponents.

    Takashi Mitsuya, like every other Toman founder, would gladly give his life for the sake of the gang and his companions. During many fights, he was observed giving it his all and single-handedly defeating a large number of opponents. Even after being hit by a rod, Mitsuya didn’t lose consciousness, and his fighting skills are even talked about among other gang members.

    Izana Kurokawa

    Despite his innocent appearance and sunny disposition, Izana’s sinister role in Tokyo Revengers makes him the most menacing of the group’s protagonists. His past had such a profound effect on him that he was rendered psychologically deranged; now, he would do everything to further his power.

    As the eighth generation Black Dragon, Izana led the Tenjiku. His combat skills were undeniable, but what truly made him dangerous was his ability to read his opponents’ thoughts and anticipate their moves. Izana, the strongest man in Tenjiku, was able to match Mikey blow for blow.

    Senju Kawaragi

    At the same time that the story features fragile female characters like Hina and Emma, it also introduces us to Senju, a prodigy of strength and drive. Before becoming a member of the Tokyo Manji Gang, Senju was one of Kanto’s Three Deities and the head of the Brahmans. Senju has all the traits necessary to be an excellent leader, but she was ultimately toppled by her brother, who wished to steer the gang in a more sinister direction.

    Senju may be diminutive in stature, but she makes the most of her nimble frame by engaging in acrobatic combat techniques. When she combines her superhuman strength with her nimble footwork, she becomes practically unstoppable. With her incredible strength, she was able to rise to the top of the ranks in both Toman and Brahman.

    South Terano

    The Three Deities Arc’s primary antagonist, South Terano headed up the Rokuhara Tandai gang. While his introduction didn’t occur until chapter 207 of the manga, his tyrannical character and vicious disposition quickly made him a fan favorite. People were terrified of him because of his short temper and a brazen willingness to kill.

    Draken was dwarfed by South’s massive frame, and South’s abilities were proportional to his size. With a single blow, he could knock out any ordinary fighter. In a one-on-one encounter during the Battle of the Three Deities, he defeated Senju; however, Mikey quickly lost his temper and made Senju taste dirt.

    Ken Ryuguji

    The Draken, Ken Ryuguyi, is the second most powerful person in Tokyo Revengers. He’s been with the Toman crew since the beginning and currently serves as the gang’s vice president. If you were to choose one member of the Toman as the sanest, it might be Draken. Despite his reluctance to show his loved ones affection, he would sacrifice all to ensure their safety.

    Draken has been through the wringer in the ring, suffering anything from a terrible knife wound to taking a violent strike from a metal rod, but that hasn’t stopped him from being a monster. Although South Terano is enormous, he can still knock it around with a few well-placed punches and kicks. Seeing him take on and vanquish a gang of enemies alone has become routine.

    Manjiro Sano

    The fact that Mikey is number one shouldn’t come as a shock. There is good cause for his nickname, “Invincible Mikey,” as he is unbeatable by any opponent. Mikey’s intensity in a battle changes the dynamic of the situation, to the point where not even his best friend, Draken, can stop him.

    Mikey usually wins with a single powerful kick that knocks out his opponent. Mikey knocked out his opponent with a single headbutt, despite taking many blows to the head from a rod. He picked up the guy who was grabbing onto his leg, then used the same leg to knock out the other guy. Draken, Peh-yan, Chifuyu, and Mitsuya all joined forces, but Mikey was still too powerful.

    Frequently Asked Questions 

    Who is the most loved anime character in Tokyo Revengers?

    Millions of viewers cast ballots to determine who they liked best among the show's diverse and intriguing cast. As the head of the Toman First Generation, Manjiro Sano has been voted the best character by the majority of the fandom.

    Who can defeat Mikey?

    Izana and Terano South are the only people I know of who could confront Mikey. Mikey has only faced off against these two, and they were the only ones who could keep up with his strength for any length of time.

    Who is stronger than Draken?

    Of Japan's three deities, South Terano presides as the head of the Rokuhara Tendai. As they were face to face, his massive frame made Draken look small.
